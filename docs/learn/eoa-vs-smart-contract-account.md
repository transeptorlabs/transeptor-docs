## Externally Owned Accounts (EOAs):

An Externally Owned Account (EOA) is the most common type of account on the Ethereum network. It is a basic account that is owned and controlled by a user, and it is identified by a public/private key pair. EOAs are used to send and receive Ether and other tokens, as well as to deploy and interact with smart contracts.

EOAs have several limitations that make them less suitable for mass adoption, including:

- They cannot receive tokens or interact with contracts without first sending a transaction.
- They cannot delegate transaction authorization to a third party.
- They are limited to paying transaction fees in Ether, which can be expensive and volatile.

## Smart Accounts (ERC-4337):

Smart Accounts are a new type of account introduced by the ERC-4337 standard. They are essentially smart contracts that are authorized to send transactions on behalf of a user. Smart Accounts can receive tokens and interact with contracts without needing to first send a transaction, and they can delegate transaction authorization to a third party. They also offer the ability to pay transaction fees in any token, not just Ether.

Smart Accounts offer several advantages over EOAs that make them more suitable for mass adoption, including:

- **Improved security**: By separating the authorization logic from the execution logic, Smart Contract Accounts reduce the risk of user error and increase security.
- **Increased flexibility**: Smart Contract Accounts allow users to pay transaction fees in any token, which can make transactions cheaper and more predictable.
- **Improved user experience**: Smart Contract Accounts can interact with contracts and receive tokens without requiring the user to send a transaction, which can improve the overall user experience and reduce friction.
